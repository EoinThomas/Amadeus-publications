  year      = {2024},
  publisher = {ACM}
}
 Abstract: The paper presents X-Ray-TLS, a technique and toolset for intercepting and decrypting TLS-encrypted network traffic in a transparent manner by extracting the TLS session keys directly from the memory of one of the communicating endpoints (with appropriate privileges). Unlike man-in-the-middle approaches, X-Ray-TLS does not require altering the TLS handshake or certificates. The authors describe implementation details and demonstrate that the method can reveal plaintext from active TLS sessions with negligible performance impact, useful for enterprise traffic monitoring or forensic analysis (when legally permitted).
